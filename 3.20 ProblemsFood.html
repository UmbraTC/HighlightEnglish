" I think her school cafeteria should offer healthier food"

Class PDF

Exercises

Listen to this people’s problems and discuss possible solutions.

  1. My husband and I are both very worried our daughter who is now 15 years old. She seldom eats foods like broccoli or carrots, apples or oranges. Instead, she usually eats unhealthy foods like hamburgers and French fries, cookies and candy. I think her school cafeteria should offer healthier food, and TV commercials should advertise food. How can we get our daughter to change her eating habits?
  2. Help! I’m a university student living from my family and home for the first time. I have to pay a lot of money for things like costs and books and transportation and rent. Yesterday, I went to the supermarket and I was so surprised by how much everything costs. The frozen pizza I bought costs dollars! Can you give me any advice on how I can spend less money on food?
  3. I would really be very thankful for advice you can give me. Our young son, who is 5 years old, is active. He loves to run and play outside with his friends, watch TV, and read books. The problem is this: our doctor says that he is too and he should eat more. However, I try to give him lots of food to eat, but he just doesn’t eat very much. He eats just a little, and then he runs away to watch his favorite cartoon or play with his friends. My friend says I should let him eat things like hot dogs and ice cream to get him more interested in eating. But I want our son to eat lots of fruits and vegetables
