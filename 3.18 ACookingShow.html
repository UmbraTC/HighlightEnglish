    • Pickled cabbage is a delicious to have on hand since it goes with just about everything. In addition to the tangy flavor, red cabbage brings a pop of bright color to a plate; when the acidic vinegar brine comes into contact with the cabbage, the goes from deep purple to a beautiful shade of red.
    • Spoon some pickled cabbage on top of fish tacos, hot dogs, and burgers, as well as pulled pork sandwiches. You can also mix it with cilantro and green onions and serve it with chicken or meat as a flavorful and colorful topping.

    • Ingredients

    • 1/2 medium head red cabbage (about 2 )
    • 1 1/4 of water
    • 1 1/4 cups of apple cider vinegar
    • 1 1/2 kosher salt
    • 2 teaspoons sugar
    • A of red pepper flakes, optional
    • 2 of garlic, sliced
    • 2 teaspoons of coriander seeds
    • 12 black peppercorns

    • Recipe

    1. Gather the ingredients.
    2. Using a large sharp knife, a mandoline, or a food processor fitted with the slicing disc, the cabbage into thin shreds. Set aside.
    3. Put the water, vinegar, salt, sugar, pepper flakes, garlic, coriander, and peppercorns in a saucepan and bring it to over high heat.
    4. Put the shredded red cabbage in a bowl and then the hot brine over it.
    5. Divide the brined cabbage 2 (1-pint) jars.
    6. Place the lids on the jars and for at least 2 hours before serving. For the best flavor, however, refrigerate for 24 to 48 hours before . This recipe can keep in the for about 7 days.
